After the game of September 2, the Cubs record was 84-52 with the Mets in second place at 77-55. But then a losing streak began just as a Mets series with Baltimore was beginning. On Saturday, the Cubs lost the final game of a series at Cincinnati, then came home to play the Pittsburgh Pirates (who would finish in third place). After losing the first two games by scores of 9-2 and 13-4, the Cubs led going into the ninth inning. A victory in Milwaukee was supposed to be a positive springboard since the Cubs were to play a crucial series with the Mets the very next day. But Willie Stargell drilled a 1-0 pitch from the Cubs' ace , Regan, onto Sheffield Avenue to tie the score in the top of the ninth. The Cubs would lose 7-5 in extra innings. Burdened by a four-game losing streak, the Cubs traveled to Shea Stadium for a short two-game set. The Mets won both games, the Cubs left New York with a record of 84-58 just 1⁄2 game in front. Disaster followed in the form of a 99 loss Phillies team nonetheless defeated the Cubs twice, to extend Chicago's losing streak to eight games. In a key play in the second game, on September 26, Phillies starter Dick Selma threw a surprise pickoff attempt into the glove of Cubs third baseman Ron Santo, who was nowhere near the bag or the ball. Selma's throwing error opened the final two innings to stop a Phillies rally. After that second Philly loss, the Cubs were 84-60 , but the Mets had pulled ahead at 85-57. The Mets would not look back. The Cubs' winning streak finally ended the next day in St. Louis, but the Mets were in the midst of a ten-game winning streak, and the Cubs, wilting from team fatigue, generally deteriorated in all phases of the game.
