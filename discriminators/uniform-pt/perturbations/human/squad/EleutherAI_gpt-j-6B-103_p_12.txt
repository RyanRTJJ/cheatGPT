A tragic blow struck Burke with the loss of his wife in August 1794, to whom he was tenderly attached, and one in whom he saw signs of promise, which were not patent to others and which, in fact, appear to have been non-existent (though this view may have rather to do with the fact that Richard Burke had worked successfully in the early Roman Catholic emancipation).
