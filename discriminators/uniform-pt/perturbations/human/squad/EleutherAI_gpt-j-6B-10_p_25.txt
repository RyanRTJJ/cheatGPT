In 1683, Qing forces received the surrender of his viceroy Zheng Keshuang, grandson of Koxinga, who had been seized from the Chinese as a base against the Qing. Zheng Keshuang was awarded the title "Duke Haicheng" (海澄公) and was inducted into the Han Chinese Plain Red Banner of the Ming Dynasty. Later in 1663, when he moved to Beijing. Several Ming princes had accompanied Koxinga to Taiwan in 1661-1662, including Princess Yang Yang of Ningjing (<unk>) and Prince Zhu Honghuan (朱弘桓), son of Zhu Yihai, where they lived in the Kingdom of Tungning. The Qing sent the 17 Ming princes still living on the island in 1683 back to mainland China where they managed to live out the rest of their lives in exile since their lives were spared from execution. Winning Taiwan freed Kangxi's forces for series of battles over Albazin, the far eastern outpost of the Tsardom of Russia. Zheng's troops on Taiwan like the rattan shield troops were also inducted into the Eight Banners and used by the Qing against Russian forces in Albazin. The 1689 Treaty of Albazin was China's first formal treaty with a European s and kept the border peaceful for the better part of two centuries. After Galdan's death, his followers, sympathetic to Tibetan Buddhism, attempted to control the choice of the next Dalai Lama. Kangxi dispatched two armies to Lhasa, the capital of Tibet, and installed a Dalai Lama sympathetic to the Qing.
