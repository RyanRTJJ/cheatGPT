The first formal school on the Idaho frontier was established at Fort Owen in Bitterroot valley in 1862. The students were Indian children and the children of Fort Owen families. The first school term started in early winter and only lasted until Feb. 28. Classes were taught by Robinson. Another early subscription school was started by Thomas Dimsdale in Virginia City in 1870. In this school students were charged $1.75 per week. The Montana Territorial Legislative Assembly had its inaugural meeting in 1864. The first legislature authorized counties to levy taxes for schools, which set the foundations for public schooling. Madison County was the first to receive its share of the newly authorized taxes and it formed fhe first public school in Virginia City in 1886. The first public school was scheduled to start on 5 January 1866, but severe weather postponed its opening until 1876 the first school year ran through the summer and didn't end until winter. One of the first teachers at the school was Sarah Raymond. She was a 25-year-old woman who had traveled to Virginia City via wagon train in 1865. To become a certified teacher, Raymond took a She went to the post office near her home and paid a $6 fee in gold dust to obtain a teaching certificate. With the help of an assistant teacher, Mrs. Farley, Raymond was responsible for teaching 40 to 60 students each day, out of the 81 students enrolled at the school. Sarah Raymond was paid at a rate of $125 per month, while Mrs. Farley was paid $75 per month. There were no textbooks used in the school. In their place was an assortment of books brought in by various emigrants. They would stop teaching the following year, but would later become the Madison Academy of schools.
