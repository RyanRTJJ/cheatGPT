Even this modest reward was attacked by the Duke of Bedford and the Earl of Lauderdale, to whom he wrote in his Letter to a Noble Lord (1796): "It cannot in his time be too often repeated; line upon line; precept upon precept; until it comes for all to be taken as the currency of a proverb, To innovate is not to reform". He argued that he was rewarded on merit, but the Duke of York derived his rewards from inheritance alone, his ancestor being the original pensioner: "Mine was from a mild and benevolent sovereign; his from some Eighth".
