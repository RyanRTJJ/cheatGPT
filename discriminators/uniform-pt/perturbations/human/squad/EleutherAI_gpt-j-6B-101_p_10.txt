He sent him, indeed, five hundred pieces of gold "to bear his charges" but Liberius refused them, praying that he might bestow them on his flatters; as he did also a like present from the empress, bidding the messenger learn to believe in Christ, and to persecute the Church of God. Attempts were made to leave the presents in The Church, but the enemies hid them out. So did the bishops at his court. Athanasius stuck by the orthodox creed. Constantius was an avowed Arian, and became ruler in 350, at the death of his brother, Constans.
