Even this modest reward was attacked by both the Duke of Bedford and the Earl of Lauderdale, to whom Burke replied in his Letter to a Noble Lord (1796): "It cannot for any time be too often repeated; line upon line; precept upon precept; until one becomes into the currency of a proverb, which is only to remind that to reform is not to reform". He argued that he was rewarded on merit, but the Duke of Bedford received his rewards from inheritance alone, his ancestor being the original pensioner: "Mine was from a mild temper sovereign; his from Henry the Eighth".
