After a win in the middle of September 2, the Cubs record was 84-52 with the Mets in second place . But then a losing streak began just as a Mets winning streak was beginning. The Cubs lost the final game of a series at Cincinnati, then came home to play the resurgent Pittsburgh Pirates (who would finish the season in first place). After losing the first two games by scores of 9-2 and 13-4, the Cubs led going into the bottom of the ninth inning. A win would be a positive springboard since the Cubs were to play a series finale with the Mets the very next day. But Willie Stargell drilled a 2-out, 2-strike pitch from the Cubs' ace reliever, Phil Regan, down the left field line at First Avenue to tie the game in the top of the ninth. The Cubs would lose in extra innings 10-2. Bolstered by a four-game winning streak, the Cubs traveled to Shea Stadium for a short two-game set. The Mets won both games, and the Cubs left New York with a record of 84-58 just 1⁄2 game in front. Disaster followed in Philadelphia, as a 99 loss Phillies team nonetheless defeated the Cubs twice, to extend his winning streak to eight games. In a key play in the second game, on September 21, 1960, Cubs starter Dick Selma threw a surprise pickoff attempt to third baseman Ron Santo, who was nowhere near the bag or the ball. The error opened the gates to a Phillies rally. After the second Philly loss, the Cubs were 84-60 and the Mets had pulled ahead at 85-57. The Mets would not look back. The Cubs' eight-game losing streak finally ended the next day in St. Louis, but the Mets would win the next three games and are in the midst of a ten-game winning streak, and the Cubs, wilting from age, generally deteriorated in all phases of the game.
