After the game of September 2, the Cardinals record was 84-52 with the Mets in second place at 77-55. But then a losing streak began , a Mets winning streak was beginning. The Cubs lost the final game of the season on Monday at Cincinnati, then came home to play the resurgent Pittsburgh Pirates (who would finish in third place). After losing the first two games by scores of 9-2 and 13-4, the Cubs led going into the ninth inning. A win against the Reds would have been a positive springboard since the Cubs were to play a crucial series with the Mets the very next day. But Willie Stargell drilled a 2-out, 2-strike pitch from the Cubs' ace reliever, Phil Regan, onto the right field wall to tie the game in the top of the ninth. The Cubs would lose 7-5 in extra innings. Burdened through a four-game losing streak, the Cubs traveled to Shea Stadium for a short two-game set. In the end the Yankees won both games, and the Cubs left New York with a record of 84-58 just 1⁄2 game in front. Disaster followed in Philadelphia, as a 99 loss Phillies team nonetheless defeated the Cubs twice, to extend Chicago's losing streak to eight games. On a key play in the second game, on September 11, Cubs starter Dick Selma threw a surprise pickoff attempt to third baseman Ron Santo, who seemed nowhere near either the plate or the ball. Selma's throwing error opened the gates to a Phillies rally. After that second Philly loss, the Cubs had won just three of eight and the Mets had pulled ahead at 85-57. The Mets would never look back. The Cubs' eight-game losing streak finally ended the next day in St. Louis, but the Mets were in the midst of their eighth straight winning streak, and the Cubs, wilting from team fatigue, generally deteriorated in all phases of the game.
