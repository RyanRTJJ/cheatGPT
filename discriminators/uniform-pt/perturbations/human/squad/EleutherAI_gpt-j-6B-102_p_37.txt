The first formal school on record was at Fort Owen in the valley in 1862. The students were Indian children and the children 's teachers were Fort Owen employees. The first school term started in early winter and only lasted until February . The students were taught by Mr. Robinson. Another early subscription school was started by Mr. Thompson in Virginia City in 1863. In this school students were charged $1.75 per week. The Montana Territorial Legislative Assembly had its inaugural meeting in 1867, becoming the first legislature authorized counties to levy taxes for schools, which set the foundations for public schooling. Madison County was the first to take advantage of the newly authorized taxes and it formed the first public school in Virginia City in 1886. The first school year , the first school year in Virginia, was scheduled to begin in 1866, but severe weather postponed its opening until March. The first school year ran through the summer and didn't end until January 1847. One of the first teachers at the school was Sarah Raymond. She was a 25-year-old widow who had traveled to Virginia City via wagon train in 1838. To become a certified teacher, Raymond took a test in her home and paid a $6 fee in gold dust to obtain a certified teaching certificate. With the help of an assistant teacher, Mrs. Farley, she was responsible for teaching 50 to 60 students each day out of the 81 students at the school. Sarah Raymond was paid at a rate of $125 per month, and Mrs. Jones and Miss Spencer were paid $75 per month. There were no textbooks used in the school. In their place were an assortment of books brought in by various emigrants. Sarah quit teaching the following year, but would later become the Madison County superintendent of schools.
