He was satisfied that the only award he would receive was probably a pension of Â£2,500. Even this modest reward was from the Duke of Bedford and the Earl of Lauderdale, to whom Burke wrote in his Letter to a Lord (1796): "It cannot at this time be too often repeated; line upon line; precept upon precept; until so many have been made into the currency of a proverb, To innovate is not to reform". He argued that he was rewarded on merit, but the Duke of Bedford received his rewards from inheritance despite his ancestor being the original pensioner: "Mine was from a mild and prosperous line, the rest of his from Henry the Eighth".
