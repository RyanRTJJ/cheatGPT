The first school on record was at Fort Owen in Bitterroot valley in 1862. The students were Indian and the children of Fort Owen employees. The first school term started in October and only lasted until February 28. Classes were taught by Mr. Henry Loomis. An early subscription school was started by Thomas Dimsdale in Virginia City in 1863. In this school students were charged $1.75 per week. The Montana Territorial Legislative Assembly had its inaugural meeting in 1864. The Assembly authorized to levy taxes for schools, which laid the foundations for public schooling. Madison County was the first locality to take advantage of the newly authorized taxes and it formed fhe first public school in Virginia City in 1886. The first school year was scheduled to begin in January 1866, but severe weather postponed its opening until March. The first school year had run, and the school's program ran through the summer and didn't end until September. One of the first teachers at the school was Sarah Raymond. She was a 25-year-old woman who had traveled to Virginia City via California in 1865. To become a certified teacher, Raymond took a test in her home and paid a $6 fee in gold dust to obtain a teaching certificate. With the help of an assistant teacher, Mrs. Farley, Raymond was responsible for teaching 50 to 60 students , out of the 450 students enrolled at the school. She was paid at a rate of $125 per month, and Mrs. Farley was paid $75 per month. There were no textbooks that were issued by the school. In place was an assortment of books brought in by various emigrants. Sarah quit teaching the following year, but would later become the Madison County superintendent of schools.
