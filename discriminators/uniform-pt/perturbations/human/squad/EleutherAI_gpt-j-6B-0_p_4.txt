After the game of September 2, the Cubs led the National League Central 84-52 with the Mets in second place with a 48-52 record. But then a losing streak began just as a Mets winning streak was starting. The Cubs lost the final game of a series against Cincinnati, then came home to face the resurgent Pittsburgh Pirates (who would finish in third place). After losing the first two games by scores of 9-2 and 13-4, the Cubs led going into the ninth . The win would be a positive springboard since the Cubs were to play a crucial series against the Mets the very next day. But Willie Stargell drilled a 2-out, 2-strike pitch from the Cubs' ace reliever, Phil Regan, onto Sheffield Avenue to tie the score in the top of the ninth. The Cubs would lose 7-5 in extra innings. Burdened by a 10-game losing streak, the Cubs traveled to Shea Stadium for a short two-game set. The Mets won both games, and the Cubs left New York with a record of 84-58 just 1⁄2 games out in front. Disaster followed in Philadelphia, as a 99 loss Phillies team nonetheless defeated the Cubs twice, to extend its winning streak to eight games. In a key play in the second game, on September 11, Cubs starter Dick Selma threw a surprise pickoff attempt to third baseman Ron Santo, who was nowhere near the ball and could not dive at the ball. Selma's elbow opened the gates to a Phillies rally. After that second Philly loss, the Cubs were 84-60 down, but the Mets had pulled ahead at 85-57. The Mets would not look back. The Cubs' eight-game losing streak ended the next day in St. Louis, but the Mets were in the midst of a ten-game winning streak, the Cubs, wilting from team fatigue, generally deteriorated in all phases of the game.
