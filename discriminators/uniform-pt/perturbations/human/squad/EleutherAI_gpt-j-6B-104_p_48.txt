Emomali Rahmon came to power after defeating former prime minister Abdumalik Abdullajanov in a November presidential election with 58% of the vote. The elections took place shortly after the end of the war, and Tajikistan was in a state of near complete devastation. The estimated dead numbered over 100,000. Around 1.2 million people lost their lives inside and outside of the country. In 1997, a ceasefire was reached between Rahmon and opposition parties under the guidance of Jean-Pierre Merrem, Special Representative to the Secretary General, a result widely praised as a successful United Nations peace keeping initiative. The majority government, with 30% of ministerial positions going to the opposition. Elections were held in 1999, though they were criticized by opposition parties and international observers as unfair and Rahmon was re-elected with 98% of the vote. Elections in 2006 were again won by Rahmon (with 79% of the vote) and he began his third term in office. Foreign parties boycotted the 2006 election and the Organization for Security and Cooperation in Europe (OSCE) criticized it, although the UN and the Commonwealth of Independent States claimed the elections were legal and transparent. Rahmon's administration came under further criticism from the OSCE in October for its systematic repression of the media. The OSCE claimed that the Tajik Government censored Tajik and foreign websites and initiated tax inspections on independent printing houses that led to the cessation of printing activities for a number of independent newspapers.
