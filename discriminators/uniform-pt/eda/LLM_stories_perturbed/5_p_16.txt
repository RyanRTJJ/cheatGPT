Jenna didn't believe him. She knew she had what it took to the craft as a great blacksmith. And so, when she was eighteen, she left home and traveled to the nearest city, where she found work as an apprentice to a master blacksmith.

The work was hard, but Jenna loved it. She learned to heat and hammer with the hammer and anvil, and she formed many beautiful pieces of art from nothing but raw materials. She also learned to work with flame, using it to heat the metal to just the right temperature before shaping it with her hammer.

Over the years, Jenna honed her skills, becoming one of the best blacksmiths in the city. People came from all around to commission her, and she used her skills to create beautiful items for them. She crafted everything from custom doors and hinges to elaborate swords and armor.

One day, a wealthy merchant approached Jenna with a special request. He wanted her to create a set of iron gates for his property that would be both beautiful and strong enough to keep out unwanted guests.

Jenna accepted the offer and promptly set to work. For weeks, she toiled over the gates, heating the metal in the forge and shaping it with her hammer. She used every trick she had learned over the years, working the metal with precision and care.

When she was done, the gates were a masterpiece. They were strong and sturdy, yet delicate and beautiful all at the same time. The merchant was thrilled with them, and paid Jenna handsomely for her work.

As Jenna watched new blacksmithing tools being installed at the mansion, she couldn't help but feel a sense of accomplishment. With just iron and flame, she had created something truly amazing. She knew that she had proved her father wrong - blacksmithing was not just a one man job.
