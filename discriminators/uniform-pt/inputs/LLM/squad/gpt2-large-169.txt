During the 1940s, Broz attended a series of conferences organized by the United Nations in order to strengthen the Communist movement in Yugoslavia and other East European countries. During this time, he was also a member of the Politburo of the Communist Party of Yugoslavia when the party was led by Lazar Mitrokhin, the leading ideologue of Communist politics.

During the 1960s, Broz was a member of the Political Bureau of the Communist Party of Yugoslavia, where he represented a minority of the party leadership. Broz was also on the Central Committee.

In his speeches there, he criticized his own party and the leadership. He wrote that Yugoslav Soviet revisionism was an unprincipled system which promoted the struggle against Western imperialism in order to weaken the forces of international Communism.
