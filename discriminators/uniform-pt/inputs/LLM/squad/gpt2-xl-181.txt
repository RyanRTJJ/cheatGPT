In 2011, Yale produced its 20th Nobel Prize, an honor given to those for who have had "exceptional service to humanity" in the fields of economics, peace, medicine, literature, and other disciplines. Among Nobel Prize winners who have received their degrees from Yale are George H.W. Bush and George W. Bush, former French President Nicolas Sarkozy, former Spanish Prime Minister Jose Maria Aznar, former Russian President Dmitry Medvedev, and former Italian Prime Minister Silvio Berlusconi.

In addition to these notable alumni from Yale's top-ranked graduate schools, the university has an extensive network of distinguished faculty, scholars, and professionals. From its renowned Yale College, for instance, it has grown to have more than 35,000 undergraduate and graduate students, as well as over 10,000 alumni. In the United States alone, the university ranks No. 9 among the most prestigious schools of all U.S. universities with 25,000 alumni.

Today, there are five faculties of medicine at Yale University: the Department of Radiology (including the Yale-New Haven Hospital School of Medicine), the Department of Internal Medicine (including the Yale Graduate School of Medicine), the Department of Surgery, the Department of Neurosurgery, and the Department of Clinical Medicine. There is also a School of Medicine devoted to the field of Clinical Mental Health.
