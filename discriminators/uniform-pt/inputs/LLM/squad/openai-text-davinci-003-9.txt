The palace is divided into four separate wings, surrounding a central quadrangular courtyard. Each wing is made up of two floors, and contains many reception rooms, state apartments, and smaller rooms that were occupied by courtiers and visiting dignitaries. The palace is made up of over two thousand rooms, with over two and a half thousand windows. The palace also has its own church, the Chapel of St. George, built in the 14th century. The grounds feature a number of individual gardens, including the private Queen's Garden.
