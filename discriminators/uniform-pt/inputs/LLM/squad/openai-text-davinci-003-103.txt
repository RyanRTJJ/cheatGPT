Burke's legacy as an important figure in British public life is apparent in his writings, speeches, and actions. For example, his acclaimed speech on the Impeachment of Warren Hastings is widely credited with helping to shape the creation of the British Empire, while his Reflections on the Revolution in France set the terms of the debate concerning the French Revolution for decades after his death. He is commemorated in a number of place names and in statues across the United Kingdom.
