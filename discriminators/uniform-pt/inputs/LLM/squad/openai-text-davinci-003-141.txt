Generally speaking, northern universities were more closely allied to the church and their focus was more on the classics while southern universities focused more on practical knowledge and skills. Northern universities were also more closely integrated within the broader culture – in other words, they attracted a broader student body – while southern universities were more exclusive and elite. Southern universities typically had a stronger emphasis on specific disciplines, such as law or medicine, while northern universities had a more general approach to education. Additionally, northern universities tended to have more women professors than in the south.
