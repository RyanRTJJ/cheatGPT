Zhejiang's industrial clusters are mainly concentrated in the manufacturing sector and in its four provinces, mainly in the Guangdong coastal areas in the north, Guangxi in the center, Henan in the east, and Ningxia in the south (Fig. 3). In 2016, the Guangdong coast and the cities of Dongguan, Guangzhou, Jiangxi, and Zhejiang together accounted for 51.3 percent of Zhejiang's total output.

As a result of its rapid economic development the Chinese economy remains a key player in the global economy and the world economy. China is the second-largest economy in the world today, after the United States. Its manufacturing sector has also emerged as a major engine of domestic economic growth.

China has become an important contributor to global industrial activity. Its exports, primarily in terms of industrial metal products, to the United States have almost doubled from 4 percent in 1998 to 20 percent in 2016. Its top customers in 2016 were United States, Europe, Japan, and Germany.

In Zhejiang, the top export destinations for raw mineral products for China were Italy, India, Germany, the United States, as well as Canada and United Kingdom.
