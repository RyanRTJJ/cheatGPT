Litvinov had served as the People's Commissar for Foreign Affairs of the Soviet Union since 1930 and was a strong advocate of the policy of collective security which had alienated Reich governments since 1933. His removal in May 1939 signaled that the Soviet Union was looking to significantly change its stance abroad, leading to increased German-Soviet cooperation and the signing of the Molotov-Ribbentrop Pact on August 23, 1939. This historical event has been widely analyzed by scholars since then, and has been viewed as a key episode in the lead up to World War II.
