In 1934, at the age of 42, Harris began what many BYU students believed was his final mission to China. Before his death Harris and his wife traveled extensively, giving more than 100 conferences across the United States, Europe, and Asia.

On April 3, 1975, Harris died of a heart attack while returning from a meeting in Shanghai, China with his friend and former college fraternity brother Richard "Dick" P. Hansen. At the time of Harris' death, Hansen was the Executive Vice President of the Church Educational System.

Undergraduate history

Franklin S. Harris was born on July 21, 1874 in Orem, Utah.  He graduated from the University of Utah in 1896 and thereafter taught schools for several years. He was a missionary for The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints in China for several months in 1900.

Franklin S. Harris met Mary Elizabeth McFall after church in 1902. The Harrises became engaged that spring and were married in Salt Lake City on June 3, 1903.  Harris became a professor at the University of Utah in 1903 and from 1904 to 1906 worked in the Deseret Academy.
