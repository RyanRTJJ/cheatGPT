In order to ensure Manchu loyalty and acceptance of their system, the Qing Dynastic government employed a form of social engineering, which changed the traditional Manchu ways and emphasised the need to conform to Han Chinese practices and Confucianism. This policy of consolidation and social engineering resulted in many Manchu learners, including Hong Taiji himself, learning the Chinese writing system, literature and statesmanship. This (combined with a series of military invasions) gave the Manchus the political, cultural, and institutional legitimacy to rule over China.
