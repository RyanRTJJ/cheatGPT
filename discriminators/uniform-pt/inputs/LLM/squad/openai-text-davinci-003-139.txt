This led to the emergence of smaller, more localized political systems and the rise of feudalism in many parts of the continent. This ultimately led to the establishment of nation-states and a modern political landscape. The end of the Roman Empire and the onset of the Middle Ages saw a shift from a centralized power to smaller, regional governments. Eventually, monarchies and other forms of monarchy-based governments were established, with their power and authority diminishing in many areas as the Middle Ages progressed.
