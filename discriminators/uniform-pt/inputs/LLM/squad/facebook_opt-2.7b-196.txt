Today, we look at some of the more unusual covers of Queen’s albums, some of which have previously remained unpublished, and some of which are on sale for the very first time.

A Night at the Opera – £25

This picture is one of the most famous in music history – but only a tiny fraction of those who saw rock’n’roll superstar Freddie Mercury perform live ever got to see him, let alone have his autograph. It was taken during one performance of “A Night at the Opera” at London’s Wembley Arena on 8th June 1975. It features Mercury’s signature on the back of his suit jacket, his “Bohemian Rhapsody” bandmates Brian May on the wings and Roger Taylor on the drums.

Bohemian Rhapsody – £6

Freddie Mercury on his first ever album with Queen, “Bohemian Rhapsody”.

It was the best of times, and it was the worst of times for Queen. With a record contract signed in September 1970, Mercury embarked on his career as a solo artist, and with “Bohemian Rhapsody” he had hit upon a formulaic pop masterpiece. The success of “Bohemian Rhapsody” and Mercury’s growing celebrity had led some in “Queen” band to feel jaded.

In May 1971, lead singer and guitarist Brian May decided to depart from the band and start a solo project, while keyboard player Roger Taylor quit the group
