Estonia places high priority on foreign investment and trade. It has pursued a policy of welcoming foreign capital and providing assistance to international companies that locate operations in Estonia. The country has one of the lowest corporate tax rates in Europe and also has low import tariffs.

Trade and investment have been key contributing factors to the country's economic growth since the 1990s. The country has moved beyond its traditional economic ties with neighbouring countries and pursuing its economic relations with the rest of the world. Estonia is the world's 20th biggest exporter of goods, trading primarily with its larger European partners. Over half of its export revenue comes from the IT, telecommunications, and electronics sectors.

As a member of the European Union, Estonia is part of the single market and the free movement of goods, services, capital, and people within the EU allows for increased investment flow. Estonia currently has trade and investment agreements with over 80 countries and is committed to furthering its ties abroad. The country is actively pursuing both bilateral and regional agreements with countries in its neighbourhood such as Russia, Ukraine, and Moldova.
