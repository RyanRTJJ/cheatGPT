When Richard eventually left England in 1190, his government was left in the hands of the justiciar Hubert Walter, who was tasked with managing the country and raising more money for the Crusade. On his journey, Richard conquered many of the Crusader strongholds, including Acre, Jaffa and Arsuf. He also forged alliances with other rulers in the region, including Saladin himself. In 1192, Richard achieved a peace with Saladin, though a lasting peace was never achieved. Richard returned to England in 1194 and spent the remainder of his reign defending his kingdom against various external threats. He died in 1199, leaving England in a stable and prosperous state.
