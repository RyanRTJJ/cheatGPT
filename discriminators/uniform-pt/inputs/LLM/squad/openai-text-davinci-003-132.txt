Napoleon used the French victory as a symbol of his own growing strength. The victory allowed Napoleon to use Austria's resources to build up his own forces and advance into the rest of Europe. In addition, Vienna's strategic location on the Danube River allowed the French forces to expand their influence in the region and underpin their hegemony in Central Europe.
