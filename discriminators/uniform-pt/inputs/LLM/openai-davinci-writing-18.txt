

“I come from a long line of fire starters,” Ukai said.



At her father’s funeral, Ochako wonders how fire and ice came to be so intertwined, so intrinsically linked in both their ancestry and the nature of their powers. She remembers catching his hand with hers when she was four and confessing to papa that she wanted to be a hero, too.



I imagine that this desire for justice and heroism must have cut him deep.



Deku never knew his father until three years ago, when he fell from grace—a once-serving UA Gym teacher caught with a student in plain clothes and a classroom cleanup in progress.



Deku could glean from the gossip and conjecture that Ochako’s father had betrayed so many people in his life. Each and every flame he touched immediately became a funeral pyre for their hopes and their grief.
