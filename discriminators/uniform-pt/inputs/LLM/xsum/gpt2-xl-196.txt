The bill also gives local councils sweeping authority to refuse construction of LGBT-friendly businesses and institutions. It imposes fines of up to 15,000 euros ($17,100) for refusing construction.

In a statement, Human Rights Watch's O'Brien said: "Discrimination in employment and for education has increased dramatically."

"People are leaving Romania because they can't get a job, and they're becoming more and more frustrated at this," she said.

The U.S. State Department, a critical ally of the European Union, issued a statement in reaction to the anti-gay measures Monday.

"With the passage of this law in Romania, the country has continued to make negative steps backwards on human rights," it said.
