Former PM Tony Abbott said he would hold a plebiscite or "people's vote" after a general election due next year. The Australian Senate committee report said it should be held sometime within a few weeks of next year's election and that Mr Abbott was entitled to have his say. It has previously been argued that a plebiscite should be held in November but now the committee's chair and former Labor leader Bill Shorten agree a plebiscite would take place in the first half of 2018. The government has said it does not support a plebiscite, arguing that it would be "ineffective".
