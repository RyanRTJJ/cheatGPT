In a statement, Ecosur said it is looking into the incident and that it is working to repair the server.

In an email to customers, the company said: “Like most of all, we are deeply sorry to hear about the issue in Ecosur which affected some of our users. But we will restore the service as soon as possible.”

The company told the BBC that it “apologizes unreservedly” for the problems being experienced by a small number of customers, calling them “unacceptable.”

The firm has apologized publicly, stating that “a short-term technical glitch” in one of its software products was to blame for the error.

In a statement, Ecosur said it is working out “all the details” to get the service back running.
