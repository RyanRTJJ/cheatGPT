Liverpool youngster Koukou Coulibaly is staying in Egypt to continue his development - just days after becoming the latest player linked with a move to the Premier League.

The 22-year-old forward, who has been capped by both Mali and Ivory Coast, joined Al Ahly on a three-and-a-half-year deal on Friday.

It ends speculation that he could be joining up with compatriots Joel Matip, Sadio Mane and Naby Keita at Anfield.

Coulibaly came close to joining the Reds in 2017.

He had been on trial at the Premier League club in the summer but did not sign due to "issues with paperwork".

The Ivory Coast international, who had been at Angers since 2015, was reported to be on the brink of signing a deal with Jurgen Klopp's side earlier this month.

He had been training with the club but Liverpool backed out of a move this week, allowing Al Ahly to swoop in and sign the striker instead.

Coulibaly said: "It was an honour for me to know that a club like Liverpool was interested, but my main priority was to find a club where I can play, and where I can develop my career.

"Al Ahly seemed to be the perfect club for me, and I am very excited to be here.

"I just want to thank everyone who has helped me make this move happen, I am sure it will be a great experience here."
