The BMA said that the Scottish Government needs to take action to tackle Scotland's poor health record, including introducing stricter advertising regulations for unhealthy products, and banning price promotions on alcohol. They also want a minimum price for alcohol to be set, as well as a levy on the price of sugary foods and drinks to help raise money for public health services. The BMA also called on the government to introduce measures to tackle unhealthy working conditions and wages, such as better protection against zero hour contracts and the right to request flexible working.
