 I woke up next to a woman I've never met. Well, I've never met her, but I remember meeting her. Physically, it was the first time I have ever encountered her. Before uttering a single word to her, I knew her name, address, favourite food, and the fact that she wears socks to bed. At a small cafe store, our eyes locked and I gave a smile. "Have we met somewhere before?" I inquired, gazing at her curiously. "Maybe. Listen, I'm waiting for someone. If you could not hit on me, that'd be great." Her reply left much to be desired. "Sorry, you just seem really familiar." I gave her a small laugh and parted ways. Exiting the store, I bumped into a younger man. He muttered an apology and I responded with another. There was a familiarity of that man as well. The sight of his face was burned into my memories. A striking recognition that made my heart skip a beat. I strolled into the street. Stores pranced their goods behind glass to entice unwary pedestrian. As I admire freshly made croissants, I focused onto my own reflection on the window. It was a striking similarity. The face I bumped into, of the young man at the coffee shop, stared back. A slight beard and some wrinkles here and there were the only difference. I blinked to clear the mirage, but the image mocked me behind that glass window. I am me. That's what I'm sure of. But what am I? Tendrils of memories slowly grabbed hold of me. I am him.
