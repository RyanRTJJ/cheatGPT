 I rise above the walls of hate, And I walk the roads of gold. For my mind is one with which you can not relate, I am wise as the world is old. -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- I see the world and what you own, But I ask why it is thine. I see the clouds in the azure skies, They are god's but shall be mine. -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- All the land that mine eyes can see, Shall be graced with my memoirs. Mine! I cry as though words would bestow me All that is here and beyond the stars. -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- I can not live without pleasure, The touch of flesh on mine. The air I breathe must feel like treasure, And my water must be stronger than wine. -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- I spurn your love for fiery hate And my flames will burn your soul. Upon your trust my malicious thirst shall slate And condemn you to hell's darkest hole. -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- The layered banquets I shall feast, Nothing I see will go to waste. I have transcended, why must you see me as a beast? Your measly portions are not deserving of a place. -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- -- I lounge in my lair and witness, The world going about their day. Effort is a failure of mental fitness, So once more in bed I shall stay.
