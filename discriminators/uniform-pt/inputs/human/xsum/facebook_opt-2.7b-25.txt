Ms Sturgeon told the programme her first approach would be to support an acceptable trade deal for the whole of the UK. She said: "We are going to work with others across the political divide to avert a hard Brexit not just for Scotland but for the whole UK. "I don't believe there is a mandate to take the UK out of the single market and I don't believe there is a majority in parliament." The first minister denied she was underestimating the difficulties of finding a solution which allowed Scotland to retain access to the single market if the UK as a whole chose to leave. "I don't suggest for a second that would be straightforward or without challenges," she said. "But in the unprecedented circumstances we're in just now, I think there's an obligation on all of us to try to work out solutions that will allow the vote in Scotland to be respected, just as I understand that Theresa May wants the vote in other parts of the UK to be respected."
