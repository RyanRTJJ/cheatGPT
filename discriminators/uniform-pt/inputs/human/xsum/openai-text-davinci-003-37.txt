The broadcaster, which shows dramas such as Downton Abbey and Coronation Street, said ratings came under pressure in 2015 from the launch of new digital channels, a strong year for the BBC and a poor performance from some programmes. Chief executive Adam Crozier said ITV had an additional 50 hours of drama in the schedules for 2016 as well as major sporting fixtures including the Euro 2016 tournament. Shares in ITV fell 3.5% to 241p in afternoon trading in London. Steve Clayton, head of equity research at Hargreaves Lansdown, said its shares had done well last year, driven by an improving advertising outlook and a series of acquisitions.
