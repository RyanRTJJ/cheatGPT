A spokesman for the staff said: "The court case is unprecedented in the UK and is a David versus Goliath battle between local staff and customers versus a FTSE 100 company." Dee Valley Water employs about 180 people and has 230,000 customers across the Wrexham and Chester areas. Customers pay an average of £145 a year for their water compared with £172 for Severn Trent. The company had outbid London investment firm Ancala, which offered about £78.5m to take it over.
